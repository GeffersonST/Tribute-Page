Carl Gustav Jung

Founder of Analytical Psychology

Here's a time line of Dr. Carl Gustav Jung life:

  • 1875 - Born in Switzerland on July 26, the son of a Shepherd.
  • 1895 - Studies medicine at the University of Basel.
  • 1900 - He is an assistant to Eugen Bleuler, chief physician at the Burghölzli (psychiatric hospital) in Zurich.
  • 1902 - Doctoral thesis: "On the psychology and pathology of so-called occult phenomena"
  • 1903 - Marries Emma. From this union are born five children.
  • 1913 - Professor at the Faculty of Medicine in Zurich; Psychology and Psychoneurosis classes.
  • 1907 - "Psychology of Early Dementia"; meeting with Freud
  • 1910 - First President of the International Psychoanalytic Association.
  • 1913 - Jung names his psychology "Analytical Psychology"; resignation from his teaching post at the University of Zurich.
  • 1921 - Publication of "Psychological Types".
  • 1923 - Tower construction near Lake Zurich; Jung befriends Richard Wilhelm (translator of "I Ching - The Book of Changes").
  • 1936 - Doctor "honoris causa" at Harvard (Massachusetts).
  • 1938 - Travel to India at the invitation of the British government; President of the International Congress of Psychotherapy, Oxford; member of the Royal Society of Medicine;
  • 1944 - Appointment to the Chair of Psychology at the Faculty of Medicine of Basel; "Psychology and Alchemy".
  • 1945 - Doctor "honoris causa" at the University of Geneva.
  • 1961 - Ten days before he died, an essay for "Man and His Symbols" ends; dies on June 6th.
